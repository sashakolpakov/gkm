# The Colimit-Cone Approach: Learning Diagrams of Behaviors, Not Solvers

This document specifies the first colimit-cone experiment on the foraging
substrate. It is written by the Architect role and reviewed by the Engineer
role; the reconciliation log at the end records corrections made before and
during implementation.

The document is self-contained: Section 2 defines every category-theoretic
and free-energy notion used, at the level of a colleague with a PhD in a
neighboring field — comfortable with mathematical definitions, not assumed to
know category theory or MDL.

First results: `cone/colimit_cone_foraging_report.md` (v1/v2 substrate
`cone_foraging.py`). All four falsification criteria of Section 8 were faced
and none fired. v3 (priced bindings, Section 11) lives in the sibling
substrate `cone_foraging_bound.py` with report
`cone/cone_bound_report.md`.

## 0. The General Principle (Architect's Framing)

This document specifies foraging experiments, but the claim is not about
foraging. It is a general thesis about few-shot induction, of which the
foraging substrate, the register-transducer substrate, the Bongard substrate,
and a future ARC-AGI-3 adapter are all *instances*. ARC-AGI-3 is a stress
test, not the goal; if the principle is right it should hold across substrates
that share no surface features.

**The general claim.** Any few-shot induction problem is the problem of
extending a partial functor along an inclusion. You observe a map `F` on a
small set of situations `A`; you must act on all situations `B ⊇ A`. The
mathematically determined extension is the left Kan extension `Lan_K F`
(Section 2.3), computed pointwise as a colimit over a comma category: the new
situation's behavior is *glued* from the behaviors of the known situations
that map into it. Three consequences are substrate-independent:

```text
1. What can be learned is fixed by the morphisms, not the objects.
   Generalization succeeds iff the true rule respects the available
   morphism structure (relations, bindings, comparisons). A generalization
   failure is a missing morphism — a representation poverty — never a
   failure of the colimit formula. (Operational test: enrich the morphisms;
   if the failure dissolves, it was representational.)

2. The learnable object is the diagram-plus-cone, not the solver.
   Searching solver space directly is searching a rigid, badly connected
   landscape (the developmental-overcapacity results). Searching the space
   of diagrams and their gluings — add a leg, drop a leg, rebind, refactor —
   is better connected, so solutions are reachable at budgets where direct
   search fails. The solver is generated by the cone, never stored.

3. Free energy is the universal selector over cones.
   A two-part code prices a cone: each shared piece once, each use a
   call-plus-binding fee. Minimizing R + lambda*C builds structure exactly
   when it pays for itself. This is the same MDL ratchet the whole
   repository runs on, lifted from single agents to libraries-with-uses.
```

**Why this is more than a metaphor.** The principle makes pre-registered,
falsifiable predictions that have now been borne out in three independent
forms in this repository, none of which is about foraging per se:

```text
prediction (general)                    instance and evidence
-------------------------------------   ----------------------------------------
shared substructure across uses is      abstraction-emergence: predicate macro
factored only when it compresses        selected under multi-task / OR-factor,
the joint code (pushout)                killed by the no-share control
behavior on unseen objects is forced    register-transducer: foreign-alphabet
by the morphism structure (naturality)  hidden-test success from relational keys
representation poverty, not search,      Bongard-LOGO: Abstract concepts
caps generalization                     unreachable without the missing predicates
the cone is a better-connected          cone-foraging: gluing search solves tasks
search space than the solver space      at budgets where inline search returns 0
```

The foraging substrates exist because they make the *cone itself*
manipulable and visible: legs, bindings, and gluings are explicit objects we
can price, ablate, and watch form. The v3 substrate (Section 11) sharpens the
instance until it resembles the ARC setting — perceive many channels, pay to
abstract over them — but the thesis would be equally tested by a
transduction or Bongard version, and the Engineer's ARC connector (Section
12) is one more instance, not a change of subject.

**Substrate-independent falsifiers.** The principle is wrong if, across
substrates: factoring fires without shared substructure (1); enriching the
morphisms does not dissolve a generalization failure (2); the cone search
space is not better-connected than solver space at matched budgets (3); or
free-energy cone selection cannot be made to track genuine reuse without
hand-tuning per substrate (4). Each substrate's local falsifiers (Sections 8,
11.6) are concrete shadows of these.

## 1. The Claim Being Operationalized

The working slogan is:

> Learn not the functor, but the colimit cone that may generate it.

Concretely: the hidden solution of a task is a "functor" — a complete,
structure-respecting mapping from situations to actions. Direct search in
solver space is search in a rigid, badly connected landscape; the
developmental-overcapacity results in this repository
(`bongard/bongard_sparse_classifier_report.md`) show that cold search
often cannot enter the minimal basin at all. The colimit-cone proposal is to
search instead in the space of *diagrams plus gluings*:

```text
diagram   D : J -> Substrate     a library of small reusable behaviors ("legs")
cone      per-task gluing maps   bindings of legs into a concrete task
solver    the mediating morphism the behavior generated by executing the gluing
```

The solver is never stored explicitly. It is generated by the cone. Mutation
and selection act on legs and gluings, which is a better-connected search
space: add a leg, drop a leg, rebind a leg, refactor two gluings through a
shared leg.

The free-energy question is whether this object can be *priced* so that
selection builds cones only when they pay. The accounting tested here is:

> Cone complexity = sum over legs with shared-leg discounting — the natural
> extension of the existing no-share accounting in
> `bongard/run_abstraction_emergence.py`.

## 2. Mathematical Background

### 2.1 Categories, functors, diagrams

A **category** `C` consists of a collection of *objects*; for each pair of
objects `X, Y` a set `C(X, Y)` of *morphisms* (arrows) `f : X -> Y`; an
associative *composition* `g . f : X -> Z` for `f : X -> Y`, `g : Y -> Z`;
and an *identity* morphism `id_X : X -> X` for every object, neutral for
composition. The standard first examples: `Set` (sets and functions), or any
directed graph closed under path composition. The morphisms, not the
objects, carry the structure: two categories with the same objects but
different morphism sets are different mathematical worlds.

A **functor** `F : C -> D` maps objects to objects and morphisms to
morphisms so that sources, targets, identities, and composition are
preserved: `F(g . f) = F(g) . F(f)` and `F(id_X) = id_{F(X)}`. A functor is
the categorical notion of "structure-respecting assignment". The force of
functoriality is that `F` cannot treat two objects differently unless the
morphism structure distinguishes them.

A **diagram** in `C` of shape `J` is simply a functor `D : J -> C` where `J`
is a small category (the *index* category). Informally: a `J`-shaped
arrangement of objects and arrows inside `C`. A pair of objects with no
arrows between them is a diagram (discrete `J`); a span `B <- A -> C` is a
diagram; a sequence `X_0 -> X_1 -> X_2 -> ...` is a diagram.

A **natural transformation** `eta : F => G` between two functors
`F, G : C -> D` assigns to every object `X` of `C` a morphism
`eta_X : F(X) -> G(X)` such that for every morphism `f : X -> Y` the square
commutes: `G(f) . eta_X = eta_Y . F(f)`. It is a morphism-of-functors that
is consistent with all the structure in `C` simultaneously.

### 2.2 Cones, co-cones, colimits

Let `D : J -> C` be a diagram.

A **co-cone** over `D` with *apex* `A` is a family of morphisms
`psi_j : D(j) -> A`, one for each object `j` of `J`, such that for every
arrow `u : j -> j'` in `J` we have `psi_{j'} . D(u) = psi_j`. Pictorially:
all the legs of the diagram map into a single apex, and every triangle
formed with a diagram arrow commutes. (A **cone** is the dual notion, with
legs `A -> D(j)` *out of* the apex; limits are universal cones, colimits are
universal co-cones. In this document "cone" follows the colloquial usage for
the co-cone of a colimit — the slogan "colimit cone" means exactly this
universal co-cone, apex plus legs.)

A **colimit** of `D` is a co-cone `(colim D, iota_j)` that is *universal*:
for every other co-cone `(A, psi_j)` over the same diagram there exists a
**unique** morphism `m : colim D -> A` (the *mediating morphism*) such that
`m . iota_j = psi_j` for all `j`. The colimit is the most efficient gluing
of the diagram: it identifies exactly what the diagram's arrows force to be
identified, and nothing more. Uniqueness of `m` is the operative clause: once
the colimit cone is in hand, a compatible family of partial maps *generates*
a total map, with no further choices.

Standard examples:

- `J` discrete (no arrows): the colimit is the **coproduct** (disjoint union
  in `Set`).
- `J` a span `B <- A -> C`: the colimit is the **pushout** — the gluing of
  `B` and `C` along their shared part `A`. The factoring
  `(A AND B) OR (A AND C) ~> define A once, call it twice` in this
  repository's abstraction experiment is exactly pushout-shaped: two clauses
  glued along a shared sub-predicate.
- `J` a sequence: the colimit is the union/direct limit of an increasing
  chain — the formal shape of a growing library.

### 2.3 Comma categories and Kan extensions

Given functors `K : A -> B` and an object `b` of `B`, the **comma category**
`(K | b)` has as objects the pairs `(a, f)` where `a` is an object of `A`
and `f : K(a) -> b` is a morphism of `B`; a morphism `(a, f) -> (a', f')` is
a morphism `u : a -> a'` of `A` with `f' . K(u) = f`. Informally: all the
ways objects of `A` map into `b`, organized by the structure of `A`.

Let `F : A -> C` be a functor we know only on `A`, and `K : A -> B` an
inclusion of `A` into a larger category `B`. The **left Kan extension**
`Lan_K F : B -> C` is the universal functor extending `F` along `K`: it
comes with a natural transformation `F => (Lan_K F) . K`, and any other
functor `G : B -> C` with a transformation `F => G . K` factors through it
uniquely. When `C` has enough colimits, the extension exists and is computed
**pointwise** by a colimit over the comma category:

```text
Lan_K F (b)  =  colim over (K | b)  of  F(a)        for (a, f) in (K | b)
```

In words: the value of the extended functor at a new object `b` is the
gluing (colimit) of the values of `F` at all known objects, indexed by the
ways those known objects map into `b`.

This is the precise content of the slogan. Few-shot generalization is a Kan
extension problem: `A` = the observed situations, `B` = all situations,
`F` = observed behavior. The extension is determined by (i) the diagram of
known partial behaviors and (ii) the morphisms available in `B` — i.e., by
the *cone data*, not by any additional per-situation choice. Two corollaries
drive the experimental design:

1. **Inductive bias lives in the morphisms.** `Lan_K F` agrees with the true
   hidden functor exactly when the truth uses no distinctions invisible to
   the morphism structure of `B`. A failure of generalization is a poverty
   of the index category — a missing predicate, a missing binding — not a
   failure of the colimit formula. (This repository already met this
   empirically: Bongard-LOGO Abstract concepts were unreachable from action
   skeletons because `convex`, `symmetric`, `thin` were not in the morphism
   vocabulary; see `bongard/bongard_logo_report.md`.)
2. **Foreign-alphabet generalization is naturality.** A solver whose rule
   key cannot see token identity is a functor on a category where tokens are
   objects only distinguishable through relations (equality, position). Its
   behavior on never-seen tokens is forced by functoriality. The exhaustive
   foreign-alphabet probes in this repository are naturality tests.

### 2.4 Free energy, description length, structure functions

Fix a space of agents/solvers `a` and an evaluation ecology. The two
ingredients are:

- **Empirical risk** `R(a)`: the observed behavioral loss (missed food,
  classification error, prediction error), an average over evaluation
  episodes.
- **Complexity** `C(a)`: a description length — the number of symbols needed
  to write the agent down in the substrate's encoding. The exact rule-cost
  conventions used by each substrate in this repository are spelled out in
  `FREE_ENERGY_EXPLANATION.md`; conceptually `C` is a computable proxy for
  Kolmogorov complexity `K(a)`, the length of the shortest program producing
  `a` (uncomputable, hence the proxy).

The **free energy** at inverse-temperature-like parameter `lambda >= 0` is

```text
F_lambda(a) = R(a) + lambda C(a)
```

The name is by analogy with thermodynamics, `F = U - TS`: risk plays the
role of internal energy, description length the role of negative entropy,
and `lambda` the conversion rate between the two currencies. Minimizing
`F_lambda` is penalized risk minimization; equivalently, by the standard MDL
(Minimum Description Length, Rissanen 1978) reading, it is two-part coding:
`C(a)` is the cost of transmitting the model and `R(a)` a stand-in for the
cost of transmitting the data given the model. A structure is admitted only
when it pays for itself — the *complexity ratchet* used throughout this
repository:

```text
mutation a -> a' is selected  iff  R(a) - R(a') > lambda [C(a') - C(a)]
```

Sweeping `lambda` rather than fixing it follows the loss-complexity
landscape viewpoint of Kolpakov, *Loss-Complexity Landscape and Model
Structure Functions* (arXiv:2507.13543). Define the **structure function**

```text
C*(R) = min { C(a) : R(a) <= R }
```

the minimum description length needed to reach risk `R`, and the optimal
free energy

```text
F(lambda) = inf_a [ R(a) + lambda C(a) ]
```

These are related by Legendre-Fenchel duality: `F(lambda)` is the concave
conjugate-style envelope of the loss-complexity frontier, and each `lambda`
selects a supporting line of slope `-1/lambda` on the `(C, R)` curve.
Sweeping `lambda` therefore traces the Pareto frontier of achievable
(risk, complexity) pairs; kinks in the frontier — values of `lambda` where
the selected complexity jumps — are phase-transition candidates, flagged in
this repository by the susceptibility-style diagnostic
`chi_C(lambda) = Var[C(a)]` over the incumbent population.

**Free energy over cones.** All of the above prices a *single* agent. The
new object here is a library plus its uses, and the natural MDL extension is
two-part coding of the cone: pay for each shared leg once, pay for each use
only a call-and-binding fee. This is precisely the "shared" accounting of
the abstraction-emergence experiment, generalized from boolean predicates to
behaviors. Section 4 makes it exact.

### 2.6 Honesty clause: what is formal and what is operational

The free-energy side is formal as stated: the accounting in Section 4 is an
exact two-part code and the selection rule is exactly `F_lambda`
minimization with a validation elbow.

The categorical side is a *design discipline*, not a verified theorem. The
"category of behaviors" is not constructed as a mathematical object; no
universal property is checked by the code; the executed gluing is a
candidate apex, not a certified colimit (Section 5, pitfall P7). The
categorical vocabulary earns its keep by forcing three design commitments
that have observable consequences: (i) bindings are explicit priced
morphisms, not ambient conveniences; (ii) generalization claims are
naturality claims and must be tested on foreign instances; (iii) transfer is
a Kan-extension-shaped computation — new tasks are solved by mapping the
existing diagram in, not by fresh search. If those consequences fail
empirically, the framing dies with them; falsification criteria are in
Section 8.

## 3. Substrate: Multi-Channel Foraging

A new substrate (`cone_foraging.py`) extends grid foraging with observation
channels and behavior calls. It is deliberately a sibling of `evo_game.py`,
not a modification of it, so existing results are untouched.

World: a small grid, a start cell, food cells, one home cell, and (for the
hazard task family, Section 9.3) hazard cells.

Observation channels:

```text
FOOD:   azimuth (9-way, as in evo_game) to the nearest remaining food; HERE if none remain
HOME:   azimuth to the home cell; HERE if standing on it
HAZARD: azimuth to the nearest hazard; HERE ("safe") if no hazard within SAFE_RADIUS = 3
```

Every machine reads exactly one azimuth symbol per decision: the azimuth of
its current channel. The initial focus is the task's default sense (R8): FOOD
for food tasks, HAZARD for food-less safety tasks, HOME otherwise. In the v1/v2
substrate, rules are channel-agnostic; the channel is execution context, not
part of the rule key. This is where naturality (Section 2.3, corollary 2) is
built in: a seek behavior cannot know which target it seeks. (Section 11
revises this for inline solvers and controllers — explicit priced bindings —
while keeping legs channel-blind.)

Machine roles and permitted actions:

```text
moves       UP, RIGHT, DOWN, LEFT, STAY           cost 1 each, consume a world step
SET_FOCUS_c switch own channel to c               inline solvers and controllers only
CALL(l, c)  run library leg l bound to channel c  controllers only, depth 1
RETURN      pop back to the caller                legs only
```

Rule key: `(state, observation)` where observation is an azimuth symbol or
the wildcard `ANY`. Exact matches take precedence over `ANY`. Unlike
`evo_game.py`, `previous_action` is *not* in the key (see reconciliation log,
R1). If no rule matches, the episode halts — same sparse-halting semantics as
the rest of the repository. Control ops (`SET_FOCUS`, `CALL`, `RETURN`) do
not consume world steps but count against a separate op budget so that
zero-step loops terminate.

A **leg** is a small FSA over the abstract interface `(substate, azimuth) ->
(actions, next_substate)` whose action set includes `RETURN` but excludes
`SET_FOCUS` and `CALL`. Its azimuth is whatever channel the call bound. A leg
is the same object whether it seeks food or seeks home: that channel-renaming
binding is the nontrivial morphism that makes this a cone rather than a
shared symbol.

A **gluing** (controller) is an FSA over the same key space whose actions
include moves, `SET_FOCUS`, and `CALL(l, c)`.

Tasks (losses are smooth where possible — normalized final distances, not 0/1
indicators — so search has a gradient; all tasks add step/bump costs and a
hazard-contact cost):

```text
forage           collect all food              loss: missed-food fraction
homing           reach the home cell           loss: normalized final distance to home
forage_then_home both, in order                loss: food term + home term
flee             end safe                      loss: normalized safe-radius shortfall
forage_flee      collect all food, end safe    loss: food term + safe term
flee_then_home   end safe and at home          loss: safe term + home term
```

Train, validation, and hidden splits are disjoint level sets, as everywhere
in this repository. Hidden levels are evaluated once after selection.

## 4. Cone Complexity: Sum Over Legs With Shared-Leg Discounting

Let `def(l)` be the description length of leg `l` (sum of its rule
complexities plus a leg overhead), `glue(t)` the description length of task
`t`'s controller where a `CALL` action costs `call_cost` instead of 1, and
`inline(t)` the plain table complexity of a no-call solver.

```text
C_shared   = sum_{legs l used by at least one task} def(l)
           + sum_t glue(t)                       # CALL costs call_cost per use

C_no_share = sum_t glue'(t)                      # CALL costs call_cost + def(l) per use
                                                 # no separate library term

C_inline   = sum_t inline(t)
```

Selection minimizes `F_lambda = sum_t train_loss(t) + lambda * C_condition`,
sweeping lambda, then chooses across lambda by the validation loss/complexity
elbow exactly as elsewhere in the repository.

In two-part-code terms: `C_shared` transmits the library once and then each
task given the library; `C_no_share` is the control code that forbids the
shared dictionary; `C_inline` forbids the dictionary entirely. In categorical
terms: `C_shared` prices the diagram plus the cone legs; `C_no_share` prices
each cone leg as if its target object had to be rebuilt per use — it is the
accounting in which colimits offer no advantage over disjoint unions.

Structural properties this accounting must have (checked in tests):

1. **Single use cannot pay.** If exactly one task calls leg `l` once, then
   `def(l) + call_cost + glue >= inline` whenever the leg body is the lifted
   inline behavior, because `def(l) ~= inline` and `call_cost > 0`. The cone
   must only form under genuine reuse.
2. **No-share kills the discount.** Charging `def(l)` per call makes every
   shared solution at least as expensive as inline. If the no-share condition
   still selects calls, the result is a syntactic artifact, not a
   description-length effect.
3. **Bindings are not free.** The binding (channel id) is part of the CALL
   action's description; `call_cost` covers the call plus its binding. A
   future version with richer bindings (coordinate remaps, object filters)
   must price them explicitly, or sharing will be inflated by contorted
   bindings.

## 5. Scenarios, Conditions, Predictions

Conditions per scenario: `inline`, `shared`, `no_share`, plus `witness` — a
hand-written minimal seek leg and hand-written gluings, charged normally. As
with the hand-written `first_equals_last` automaton, the witness is a
representability floor, not an expected search outcome.

The `shared` and `no_share` conditions are populations over the *joint*
object (library + gluings); inline solvers remain available to them, so a
cone is selected only when free energy prefers it.

```text
scenario      support tasks                        transfer task        prediction
single        forage                               homing               no leg selected; shared == inline
multi         forage + homing                      forage_then_home     seek leg selected in shared; not in no_share
two_phase     forage_then_home                     homing               NO leg selected (P1; see Section 11 for the v3 reversal)
multi_seq     forage + homing + forage_then_home   (none)               leg selected WITH a RETURN boundary (Section 9.1)
```

Beyond the staged conditions, `--joint` adds `shared_joint` / `no_share_joint`
(cold co-evolution of leg bodies and gluings, Section 9), and `--growth` adds
the staged library-growth conditions (Section 9.3).

Predictions in detail:

- `single/shared`: the only candidate leg is the lifted forage champion; one
  use cannot amortize `def`, so selection returns the inline solver.
- `multi/shared`: one seek leg, two one-rule gluings (`CALL(seek, FOOD)`,
  `CALL(seek, HOME)`). `def + glue_1 + glue_2 < inline_1 + inline_2`. This is
  the pushout: two tasks glued along a shared seek behavior.
- `multi/no_share`: `def` charged per call; reverts to inline.
- `two_phase/shared`: within-task factoring is predicted NOT to pay in this
  substrate (pitfall P1). This scenario is the analogue of `or_control` in
  the abstraction experiment, not of `or_factor`.
- `transfer` after `multi`: with the library frozen and the definition
  already paid, marginal shared cost of a new task is a one-or-two-rule
  controller versus a full inline automaton. Report marginal complexity
  (library not re-charged, matching the repository's transfer protocol) and
  — the genuinely new measurement — *budget-matched discovery rates*:
  replicated small-budget searches for the transfer task with gluing search
  versus inline search. The cone claim predicts the gluing search succeeds at
  budgets where inline search fails, because the search space is the comma
  category (which legs map in, under which bindings), not raw solver space.

## 6. Known Pitfalls (Architect's Register)

**P1 — The substrate already shares within a task.** Rules are
channel-agnostic and `SET_FOCUS` is available to inline solvers, so an inline
`forage_then_home` automaton can reuse its seek rules across both phases by
switching focus once: seek rules + one `(s, HERE) -> SET_FOCUS_HOME` rule.
Within-task factoring pressure is therefore approximately zero by
construction. This is not a bug: it says the implicit colimit (channel
abstraction) is already part of the substrate, and the explicit cone
accounting is tested where it genuinely bites — *across* genomes, where
inline solvers cannot share rules. The honest prediction for `two_phase` is
"no macro", and a within-task factoring result must wait for a substrate
without free rebinding.

**P2 — God legs.** Shared-leg discounting charges a leg once regardless of
size, so selection could favor one bloated leg absorbing every behavior. The
loss term constrains this only if tasks genuinely differ. Diagnostic: report
per-leg `def` and the number of distinct callers; a single leg with one
caller and large `def` surviving selection is an accounting failure.

**P3 — Return semantics can leak behavior.** If legs returned automatically
on `HERE`, the call mechanism itself would supply "seek until arrived"
control flow for free, polluting the accounting. Legs therefore terminate
only by an explicit, costed `RETURN` action; a leg with no matching rule
halts the episode, exactly like every other machine in the repository.

**P4 — Single-use discounting.** Handled by construction (Section 4,
property 1) but must be tested, not assumed, because the lifted leg's `def`
includes the added `RETURN` boundary rules and is *not* exactly `inline`.

**P5 — Search failure masquerading as accounting failure.** If gluing
evolution never discovers `CALL`, `shared` degenerates to `inline` and the
experiment reports "no cone" for the wrong reason. The runner must report
whether any call-using genome appeared on the final Pareto set, and the
witness condition bounds what was representable.

**P6 — Op-budget loops.** Zero-step control ops (`SET_FOCUS` loops at home,
repeated calls) could loop forever; a separate op budget terminates the
episode without distorting the step-cost term.

**P7 — The executed gluing is not a certified colimit.** The code never
verifies a universal property; it executes one co-cone and prices it. Several
non-isomorphic cones can fit the same training behavior (non-uniqueness), and
free energy plus validation is the tie-break. The categorical reading is a
discipline on the design (priced bindings, naturality tests, transfer as
extension), not a theorem about the artifact; see Section 2.6.

## 7. Search Procedure

```text
stage A  per task: evolve inline champions (genetic loop, training free energy)
stage B  lift candidate legs from single-phase inline champions:
         copy rules; add (s, HERE) -> RETURN boundary rules for reachable states
         (the encapsulation mutation: same epistemic status as
         macros_from_repeated_inline_branches in the abstraction experiment —
         encapsulation after inline competence, not cold predicate invention)
stage C  per condition, per lambda, per candidate leg set: evolve gluings
         (controllers with CALL available, legs frozen), with the
         condition's own accounting inside the selection objective
stage D  free-energy selection per lambda over {inline, gluings x legs};
         validation elbow across lambdas; hidden evaluation once
stage E  transfer: freeze the selected library; evolve the transfer task's
         gluing and inline solver at matched budgets; report marginal
         complexity and replicated discovery rates
```

## 8. Falsification Criteria

The cone accounting is wrong, or the substrate inadequate, if:

1. `shared` selects a leg in the `single` scenario (single-use discount leak).
2. `no_share` selects calls anywhere (discount is not the causal factor).
3. `multi/shared` does not select the seek leg despite both tasks reusing the
   behavior (accounting fails to reward genuine reuse, or stage-C search
   failed — distinguish via P5 diagnostics and the witness condition).
4. Transfer shows no marginal-complexity or discovery-rate advantage
   (the cone buys description length but not search — half the claim dies).

### 9.3 Library growth as a sequential colimit

```text
phase 2  freeze the legacy leg (definition already paid; free_legs accounting),
         cold-evolve ONE new leg plus gluings on the flee family, under:
```

Predictions: phase-2 gluings call the legacy leg under the HOME binding


The most consequential item is Section 4 property 3. The clause "bindings are
not free ... a future version with richer bindings must price them
explicitly" was written in v1 and never exercised: in v1/v2 the binding
(which channel fills a leg's slot) is bundled into the flat `call_cost`, so
rebinding a leg from FOOD to HOME is free. v3 makes the binding an explicit
priced morphism. This is also the listed follow-up "a substrate without free
rebinding, so the true or_factor analogue becomes testable" and the step
toward ARC-AGI-3 reality.

### 11.1 The problem v3 fixes

Pitfall P1 and reconciliation R3 established that v1/v2 cannot test
*within-task* factoring: an inline `forage_then_home` solver writes its seek
rules once (channel-blind) and flips target with a single free
`SET_FOCUS_HOME` rule, so the seek body is shared within the genome for free.
The explicit cone can only win *across* genomes (scenario `multi`), never
within one (scenario `two_phase`), and `two_phase` was reclassified as an
`or_control` analogue, not the `or_factor` analogue we actually want.

The root cause is **ambient channel binding**: the focus channel is a mutable
register, switched for free, and rules are blind to it. That same
channel-blindness is what makes legs *natural* (a seek leg cannot know its
target, so it generalizes to any binding — Section 2.3 corollary 2). So we
cannot simply remove channel-blindness globally; that would destroy the
naturality story. The resolution is an **asymmetry**:

```text
machine type      channel binding          rule key                  rebinding
legs              abstract slot, bound      (substate, azimuth)       caller-supplied, channel-blind
                  by the caller (UNCHANGED)                           => naturality preserved
inline solvers    perceived, in the key     (state, channel, azimuth) NONE: no SET_FOCUS register;
controllers       (NEW in v3)                                         each rule names its channel
```

A leg keeps its channel-blind slot interface — it is the abstract,
reusable, natural object. Inline solvers and controllers lose the free focus
register: a rule now *names the channel it perceives*, exactly as an
ARC rule keyed on object colour names its colour. To act on two channels
inline you must write two sets of rules. Duplication is now real, so
factoring it out through a slot-based leg can finally pay within a single
task. The CALL that supplies a leg's binding is **priced** (`binding_cost`
per bound slot), discharging Section 4 property 3.

### 11.2 ARC-AGI-3 correspondence

This is the point of contact with the target benchmark. In an ARC game the
agent perceives many objects/colours at once; "what I do to red cells" and
"what I do to blue cells" are *different rules keyed on different colours* —
they do not share a symbol unless the agent builds a parameterized
abstraction over colour. The v1/v2 ambient focus was a convenience that
hid exactly this: it let one channel-blind rule-set serve every target for
free. The v3 keyed perception is the honest model — abstraction over the
"colour slot" must be constructed (a leg with a slot) and paid for (a priced
binding), which is precisely the structure an ARC solver would need.

### 11.3 Substrate (sibling module `cone_foraging_bound.py`)

A sibling of `cone_foraging.py`, not a modification (matching the
`evo_game -> cone_foraging` precedent), so v1/v2 results stay reproducible.
Legs reuse `cone_foraging.ConeGenome` unchanged (channel-blind). The new
top-level rule is

```text
BoundRule: (state, channel, observation) -> (actions, next_state)
```

`channel in {FOOD, HOME, HAZARD}` is the channel this rule perceives;
`observation` is an azimuth or `ANY` on that channel. There is no focus
register and no `SET_FOCUS` action. Matching is deterministic: in the current
state, scan channels in index order, taking exact-observation rules before
`ANY` within each channel; the first match fires. An `ANY` observation
always matches (every channel always yields a reading, `HERE` included), so
`(s, FOOD, ANY) -> CALL(seek, HOME)` is an unconditional dispatch rule.
`CALL` and `RETURN` action encodings are reused; `SET_FOCUS` is simply absent
from the allowed action sets.

### 11.4 Accounting (priced binding)

```text
inline/controller rule cost   = rule_overhead + sum action costs
CALL action cost              = call_cost + binding_cost      # binding_cost is NEW
leg definition (shared)       = leg_overhead + sum rule costs # charged once
```

`binding_cost` (default 0.5) is the price of the binding morphism: which
channel fills the leg's slot. The ablation `binding_cost = 0` recovers free
bindings and is a control — if within-task factoring only pays when bindings
are free, the result is a binding-pricing artifact, not genuine compression.

### 11.5 Scenarios and predictions (stated before running)

```text
scenario        task(s)              prediction (vs v1/v2)
two_phase_bound forage_then_home     shared SELECTS the seek leg (REVERSAL of v1/v2):
                (single task)        inline must duplicate the seek body per channel,
                                     so def(leg) + 2 priced calls < 2x inline seek body.
                                     This is the true or_factor analogue.
single_bound    forage (single task) no leg: one channel, no duplication to factor (control)
no_share        any                  no leg: per-call def charge kills the discount (control)
binding_free    two_phase_bound      ablation: binding_cost=0; factoring may over-fire (control)
```

Two measurement modes, mirroring the abstraction-emergence experiment, which
establishes accounting claims by candidate enumeration and uses search only
for behavior:

1. **Accounting mode (clean):** fix a seek leg (witness or lifted), construct
   the best inline solver and the cone for the single task, compare free
   energy directly. Isolates the accounting from search noise. The headline
   `or_factor` claim lives here.
2. **Evolved mode (honest):** evolve inline and gluing solutions at matched
   budgets, compare selected free energies, and report the R9 loss-confound
   if inline search fails to solve (the enlarged keyed search space is
   harder, so this is expected and must be flagged, not hidden).

### 11.6 Falsification criteria (v3)

1. `two_phase_bound/shared` does NOT select the leg in accounting mode
   (priced-binding factoring fails to pay despite real duplication — the v3
   thesis is wrong).
2. `single_bound/shared` selects a leg (one channel cannot duplicate; a leg
   here is a discount leak).
3. `no_share` selects calls anywhere (discount is not causal).
4. The factoring only appears under `binding_free` (it is a binding-pricing
   artifact, not compression).

## 12. ARC-AGI-3 Connector (one instance, not the goal)

Per the Section 0 framing, ARC-AGI-3 is one instance of the general principle.
The offline connector `arc_agi3_adapter.py` (report-free; see its module
docstring and tests) makes the correspondence concrete and runnable without a
live API.

API shape used: a frame is a 64x64 grid of 4-bit colour indices (0-15);
actions are `ACTION1..ACTION5` (simple) and `ACTION6` with `(x, y)`; each
`RESET`/`ACTION` returns a snapshot with the latest frame(s), cumulative
score, and game state. Humans solve ~100% of environments; frontier systems
under ~1% (ARC Prize, 2026) — the gap is exactly the few-shot-induction gap
this thesis targets.

The correspondence (the load-bearing claim):

```text
ARC-AGI-3                                cone substrate (v3, cone_foraging_bound)
---------------------------------------  ----------------------------------------

the avatar object                        the agent position
nearest object of colour c               the target on channel c
azimuth to that object; HERE if on it    the channel-c observation

"do the same thing to red and blue"      one channel-blind leg, two priced calls
```

The connector provides: the **scene functor** `extract_scene` (frame ->

`CALL(seek, goal_colour)` — and the bound-cone witness solves the projected

What is faithful and what is a projection, stated for honesty: the scene
functor and the colour-slot/channel correspondence are faithful and are the
intended ARC perception model. The avatar heuristic (smallest singleton, or a
declared colour) is a heuristic, not a guarantee. `scene_to_cone_level` is a
faithful projection for navigate-to-target games and an explicit
simplification for richer games (it collapses a scene to a single goal); the
general path is to run cone rules directly on `slot_observation` over all
colour slots, which `run_seek_leg_on_game` already demonstrates for a single
leg. `ACTION6` coordinate actions and hidden-dynamics induction are out of

**Can it run on a live instance?** The connector includes `ArcEnv`, a

## 13. Goal Induction (the missing core, built in foraging first)

Everything through Section 12 assumes the task is given: the loss function
names what to optimize. The general principle (Section 0) does not require
that — and ARC-AGI-3 forbids it, exposing only a scalar score. Goal induction
is the agent inferring *what the reward rewards* and then building a cone that
achieves it. Per the user's sequencing, it is built first in foraging (the
familiar context) and designed to lift to any substrate; report
`cone/cone_goal_induction_report.md`, module `cone_goal_induction.py`.

### 13.1 The general statement

A hidden task assigns reward `r` to an outcome. The agent observes a vector of
outcome features `f` (things it can measure about a trajectory) and the scalar
`r`, never the task's identity or its loss decomposition. Assume the reward
tracks the satisfaction of some unknown subset `S*` of features. Then goal
induction is free-energy model selection over feature subsets:

```text
G*(S) = argmin_S   misfit( predict r from S, across probe interactions )  +  lambda * |S|
```

— exactly the MDL feature/predicate selection this repository already runs
(Bongard, abstraction), but with the data coming from *interaction reward*
rather than labels, and the output *compiled into a cone* (which legs, bound
to which slots, in what order) rather than a classifier. The three pieces are
each an instance of an existing repository idiom:

```text
goal-induction piece          existing idiom it instantiates
----------------------------  ------------------------------------------------
infer S* from (f, r)          MDL feature selection under free energy
choose informative probes     archive_disagreement / learning_progress (thesis 9)
compile S* to a behavior      the colimit cone: legs glued under bindings
```

So goal induction is not a new mechanism bolted on; it is the same free-energy
selection turned on the reward signal, with the cone as the thing it builds.

### 13.2 Why this is the load-bearing generalization

Consequences 1-3 of Section 0 concern *how to represent and search* for a
solution once you know what you are solving. Goal induction supplies the
"what." It closes the loop the thesis (Section 9) only sketched: the agent
need not be handed a task; it can infer the task's objective from a scalar and
then deploy the cone machinery. This is what makes the whole programme
applicable to an environment that refuses to state its goal — which is the
defining property of ARC-AGI-3 and of most real problems.

### 13.3 What foraging showed (and the general lesson)

Five of six hidden foraging goals were induced exactly from 6-12 probe
episodes, and every compiled cone solved its held-out levels. The sixth case
is the general lesson in miniature: when two goal hypotheses are
reward-equivalent under the observed interactions (in foraging, "reach home"
versus "reach home and be safe", because home is generated away from hazards),
free energy commits to the simpler one, and only a distinguishing situation or
lower `lambda` separates them. A `lambda` sweep traces this directly — the
structure function of goal induction. The substrate-independent statement:

> An agent that infers goals by free energy will adopt the simplest objective
> consistent with the rewards it has seen. Under-determined goals are a
> property of the interaction history, not a bug; they are resolved by
> exploration that distinguishes the candidates, or accepted when the
> distinction does not affect reward.

This is the disagreement-driven-exploration hook (Section 0, consequence 2)
re-read as goal disambiguation: explore where candidate *goals* disagree, not
just where candidate *solvers* disagree.

### 13.4 Lifting to ARC (done)

The lift is built and demonstrated: `arc_goal_induction.py`, report
`arc/arc_goal_induction_report.md`. The same free-energy induction
core runs over the ARC connector's scene features and a hidden game score:

```text
foraging                       ARC lift
channel FOOD/HOME/HAZARD       colour slot (an object colour)
feature food/home/safe         predicate@colour atom: clear@c, avoid@c
hidden task loss               hidden game score (scalar)
seek/flee leg (channel-blind)  the SAME legs, bound to a colour slot
```

The induction core (`goal_free_energy`, `induce_goal`, `discrimination`) was
generalized to an arbitrary feature-key set and is *literally reused* — only
the substrate adapters differ. Result: across five hidden objectives over a
six-atom colour vocabulary (clear/avoid x three colours, with distractors),
the agent infers the exact objective — both the relevant colour and the
relevant relation — from 6-24 probe episodes, and every compiled cone solves
all held-out game instances. Distractor colours are correctly excluded.

What foraging handed the agent for free — the feature vocabulary — is exactly
what the ARC scene functor must supply. Section 13.5 removes that last
hand-holding for the colour-atom vocabulary.

### 13.5 Discovering the atom vocabulary from raw frames (done)

The atoms are no longer handed to the agent. `arc_scene_atoms.py` (report
`arc/arc_scene_atom_discovery_report.md`) discovers them from raw
frames in three acts:

```text
1. avatar discovery   which object translates by the action delta when I act
                      (the first cone any ARC agent must learn; not hardcoded)
2. colour discovery   the non-background, non-avatar colours present in frames
3. atom discovery     instantiate the relation schemas on discovered colours,
                      then prune to the atoms that VARY under exploration
                      (the susceptibility / informativeness idiom)
```

Goal induction then runs over the discovered vocabulary, reusing the same
free-energy core. Result: across the five hidden objectives, the avatar is
discovered (never read from the environment — a test discovers a non-standard
avatar colour), the colours are read from frames, the 9 instantiated atoms are
variance-pruned to 5-8 live atoms, and the goal is recovered with both name
match and cone match, every compiled cone solving all held-out instances.
Discovery did not cost correctness versus the oracle vocabulary
(`--oracle-vocabulary` runs the hand-given path for comparison).

What remains given is the relation SCHEMA library {clear, reach, avoid} — the
legitimate inductive bias of the morphism vocabulary (Section 0, consequence
1: what can be learned is fixed by the morphisms). Discovering the schemas
themselves from frame deltas (containment, alignment, count-equality, ...) is
the deeper frontier; the connector's `SceneDelta` (moved/appeared/vanished) is
its natural starting point. The remaining steps to a live game are schema
discovery (perception) and transport (R12 gap a), not the induction loop or
the atom vocabulary, which are complete.

## 14. General Method, Specific Connectors (Architect)

A live test sharpened the architecture. Scanning three real ARC games — `su15`
(click), `g50t` (keyboard), `sb26` (keyboard_click) — with a perception-driven
agent that clicked object centroids or pressed arrows produced **no world
change on any** (`0/3`, `max_changed_cells = 0`). The lesson is not that the
games are unsolvable; it is that *"click the centroid" and "arrow moves the
avatar" are connector decisions, and the wrong ones here*. They must not live
in the method. This section fixes the boundary.

### 14.1 The contract

The colimit-cone **method** is substrate-agnostic. It consumes an abstract
**Environment** and never names a substrate primitive (no ARC click, no
foraging move, no colour). A **connector** is the specific half: it *provides
the actions*, the perception, and the reward, adapting one substrate to the
method.

```text
Environment (the contract a connector implements):
    reset()                 -> begin / restart an episode
    actions()  -> [Action]  CONNECTOR PROVIDES THE ACTION PRIMITIVES (opaque to the method)
    apply(action)           -> advance the world by one connector-defined primitive
    features() -> {str:float} perception: observation -> named scene features in [0,1]
    reward()   -> float     the scalar the method optimizes (hidden goal)
    done()     -> bool
```

`Action` is **opaque to the method** — a handle the connector defines and
interprets. The method composes the provided actions into cones and selects by
free energy; it never constructs an action itself. This is the categorical
reading made operational: the connector supplies the morphisms (Section 0,
consequence 1 — *inductive bias lives in the morphisms*), the method glues
them.

### 14.2 What is general vs specific

```text
GENERAL (method; cone_method.py + the reused free-energy core):
  - free-energy goal induction over features()      (cone_goal_induction core)
  - disagreement-driven probe selection
  - composing provided actions into cones; selection by F = R + lambda*C
  - the discovery LOOP shape (probe -> features -> induce -> compose)

SPECIFIC (connector):
  - WHICH actions exist and what they do
      foraging:  move/seek/flee/CALL legs (cone_foraging, cone_foraging_bound)
      ARC:       ACTION1-5, and ACTION6 = click(x,y) at a perceived object
      synthetic: GoalGame seek/flee phases (a TEST connector, clearly labelled)
  - the perception map observation -> features (e.g. ARC scene functor)
  - the reward (ARC game score; foraging task progress)
```

### 14.3 Consequences and honesty

- The `0/3` real-game result is a *connector* gap, not a method gap: the ARC
  connector's action set (generic centroid-clicks / arrows) is not the action
  vocabulary these games reward. Discovering or being given the right action
  primitives is connector work; the method is unchanged.
- The method is now testable against a tiny mock Environment with no substrate
  at all (cone_method tests), proving generality directly.
- Foraging, the synthetic grid, and real ARC are three connectors behind one
  method. Renaming was avoided (R17); this boundary is the principled version
  of that same honesty — the method makes no ARC claim, and each connector
  states exactly what it provides.
